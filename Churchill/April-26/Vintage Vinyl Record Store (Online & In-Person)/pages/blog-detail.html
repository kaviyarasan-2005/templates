The Ultimate Guide to Caring for Your Vinyl Collection

Maya Johnson
Mar 28, 2026 · 8 min read
Care Guide

Your vinyl collection is an investment — not just financially, but culturally and emotionally. Proper care ensures your records sound great for generations. Here's everything you need to know.

1. Storage Fundamentals

Store records vertically, like books on a shelf. Never stack them horizontally — the weight causes warping and ring wear on covers. Keep them in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight and heat sources.

  • Temperature: 65-70°F (18-21°C) is ideal
  • Humidity: 45-50% relative humidity
  • Avoid attics, garages, and basements
  • Use proper shelving rated for the weight

2. Handling Your Records

Always handle records by the edges and the label area. The oils from your fingers leave residue in the grooves that attracts dust and degrades sound quality over time.

"The biggest enemy of vinyl isn't time — it's careless handling. Treat each record like the irreplaceable artifact it is." — Robert Kelley, Vintage Vinyl Founder

3. Cleaning Best Practices

Regular cleaning is essential. Use a carbon fiber brush before each play to remove surface dust. For deeper cleaning, consider an ultrasonic cleaning machine or bring them to our professional cleaning service.

Never use household cleaners, alcohol, or tap water on your records. These can damage the vinyl and leave residue. Use only distilled water and purpose-made cleaning solutions.

4. Inner Sleeves Matter

Replace paper inner sleeves with polyethylene-lined or rice paper sleeves. Standard paper sleeves create micro-scratches over time and shed fibers into the grooves.
