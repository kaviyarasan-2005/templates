Professional Book Appraisal

Authoritative valuations by certified bibliographers — for insurance, estate, or sale purposes.

What Is a Professional Book Appraisal?

A professional rare book appraisal is a scholarly and market-informed assessment of a volume's monetary value, historical significance, and physical condition. Our certified appraisers — members of the Antiquarian Booksellers' Association of America (ABAA) and the International League of Antiquarian Booksellers (ILAB) — combine deep bibliographic knowledge with current market data to produce authoritative valuation reports.

Unlike casual estimates or online price lookups, a professional appraisal considers dozens of factors: edition and printing sequence, binding condition, provenance, association copies, inscriptions, dust jacket presence and condition, and current market comparables from recent auction results worldwide.

Our Appraisal Process

  1. Initial Consultation: We discuss your book(s) and why you need an appraisal (insurance, estate, donation, sale).
  2. Physical Examination: Our specialist examines the volume in person or from detailed photographs.
  3. Bibliographic Research: We consult auction records, dealer catalogues, and bibliographic databases.
  4. Market Analysis: Comparable sales from Christie's, Sotheby's, Heritage Auctions, and specialist booksellers.
  5. Written Report: A comprehensive, signed document suitable for legal and insurance purposes.
