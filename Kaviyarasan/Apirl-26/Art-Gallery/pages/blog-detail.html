Behind the Scenes: Preparing for 'Abstract Horizons'

Dr. Marcus Webb, Head Curator
• Oct 15, 2026 • 5 min read

Table of Contents

  • The Initial Concept
  • Selecting the Artists
  • Spatial Design Challenges
  • Looking Forward

Curating an exhibition of the magnitude of Abstract Horizons is a journey that begins months, sometimes years, before the first artwork is hung on our gallery walls. As we finalize the installation for our winter season opening, I wanted to pull back the curtain and share the intricate process of bringing this vision to life.

The Initial Concept

The seed for Abstract Horizons was planted during a studio visit in Berlin nearly two years ago. I was struck by how artists from different backgrounds were intuitively gravitating toward similar abstract languages to express deeply personal emotional landscapes in an increasingly chaotic world.

The core question emerged: How does abstract art function as an emotional anchor today? We wanted to explore works that weren't just visually striking, but conceptually rigorous—pieces that invite viewers to project their own internal horizons onto the canvas.

"The core question emerged: How does abstract art function as an emotional anchor today?"

Selecting the Artists

Narrowing down the final selection to 8 artists was perhaps the most challenging phase. We sought a balance between established voices who have defined contemporary abstraction, and emerging talents who are pushing its boundaries.

Spatial Design Challenges

Working alongside our exhibition designers, we treated the gallery space itself as a canvas. The flow between the intense, saturated canvases of Lucia Feretti and the stark, minimalist bronze sculptures of Kenji Nakamura required careful choreography.

We constructed three temporary angled walls to create intimate viewing pockets within our main hall, allowing the larger gestural works to breathe without overwhelming the more delicate pieces.

Looking Forward

As we apply the final lighting adjustments and prepare to open the doors, the dialogue between these 24 artworks is finally audible. It's a conversation about color, shadow, and the infinite possibilities of form.

We invite you to join this conversation when Abstract Horizons opens to the public next week.
