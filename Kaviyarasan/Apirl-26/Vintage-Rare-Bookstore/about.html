Our Story

Four decades of passion, expertise, and dedication to preserving the world's most precious literary works.

Est. 1978

A Legacy Built on Literary Passion

Founded by retired Cambridge professor Edmund Hartley in 1978, VintStore began as a modest corner shop on Antiquarian Lane in London's Bloomsbury district.

What started as a personal passion project — a curated selection of Victorian first editions — quickly gained reputation among the city's literary elite. Word spread through academic circles, private libraries, and auction houses that a new kind of bookstore had arrived: one that treated every volume as a cultural treasure deserving the highest standards of scholarship and care.

Today, under the stewardship of Edmund's granddaughter, Dr. Catherine Hartley, we maintain that same rigorous dedication. Our team of eight specialists — covering everything from medieval manuscripts to 20th-century literature — brings collective expertise exceeding 140 years to every client relationship.

We believe that rare books are not merely objects, but portals. Each cracked spine and foxed page carries the fingerprints of history — and our mission is to ensure those connections endure for generations to come.
